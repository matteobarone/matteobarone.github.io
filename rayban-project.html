Brief

Ray-ban asks to review ray-ban.com/usa design with the goal of maximizing user experience and revenues during the Holiday Season.

The redesign should aim to solve ray-ban.com most important issues and to leverage on Ray-Ban strategy to accomplish the goal.

They ask to focus on a luxurious and premium experience, achieved by great content storytelling, visual consistent brand identity, and no need to say it needs to be attractive.

User Experience

Since the project was really wide, we decide to focus our user experience redesign in some keypoints:

Submenu: decreased the amount of options and organised them logically into sections (for desktop). Navigate logically the users with the goal to narrow down the results, because ray-ban has a large ammount of products (for mobile).

Product grid: allows users to shop directly from the category page or add several items to the bag and sort them later.

Filters: visible at all times on the left (for desktop). Narrowing all the avilable combinations quickly and easily.

Product page consistency: same layout for each product page, with the same number of pictures. The color variations are under the product, showing both the color of the lenses and the frame.

Smooter navigation: smart suggestions: models with the same frame or the same lenses are shown to avoid pogosticking.

Visual Style

Hierarchy: the content is arranged vertically and grouped in sections (e.g. “sunglasses”and “customize”) Different font sizes show which items have more importance.

Editorial style: assymetric layout based on a grid that allows it to be dynamic but still structured.

Fresh and clear: text overlapping with images. A lot of white space, making the site look trendy.

Campaign line

#shareheritage

The campaign is enhanced by the virtual mirror feature, where you can upload pictures of your loved ones and select the best glasses for them.

Using images of family members (including celebrities and their children) to inspire users to buy sunglasses for themselves and their family members.

Suggesting sunglasses pairings (e.g. Aviator classic + Aviator junior).
